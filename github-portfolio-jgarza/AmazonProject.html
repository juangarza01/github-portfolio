During my time at Amazon, I led the end-to-end design of a dashboard component for internal users. The goal was to reduce friction, clarify workflows, and improve efficiency across teams that operate globally.

While the full project details are confidential, this case study explores my process ‚Äî from stakeholder alignment and research planning to interaction design and iterative testing.

üìä Research Area

Stakeholder Interviews

Collaborated with cross-functional teams to understand the challenges faced by internal users. Conducted interviews to gather insights into their workflows and pain points.

These discussions informed the direction of the project, ensuring that the solutions proposed would address real user needs.

User Research

Conducted user research sessions to observe how internal tools were utilized in daily operations. Identified common friction points and areas for improvement.

The findings highlighted the need for a more intuitive interface and streamlined workflows to enhance productivity.

Key Insights

  • Efficiency: Users required quicker access to frequently used features.
  • Clarity: There was a need for clearer navigation paths within the tool.
  • Customization: Users desired the ability to tailor the interface to their specific workflows.

üé® Design Area

Design Goals

Based on the research insights, I defined three core goals for the redesign:

  • Simplify Navigation: Reduce the number of clicks to complete frequent actions.
  • Enhance Clarity: Prioritize information hierarchy and legibility.
  • Improve Consistency: Align components with internal design guidelines.

Wireframes & Flows

Created low and mid-fidelity wireframes to test layout ideas, focusing on user flow clarity and logical grouping of tasks. Feedback from team walkthroughs helped refine flow structure before high-fidelity mockups.

Final UI Overview

The final design prioritized functionality and clarity. I implemented a modular layout, responsive elements, and clear visual cues to guide users through complex processes with confidence.

While visual examples are protected by NDA, the outcome reflects Amazon‚Äôs internal standards and usability benchmarks.

üß™ Testing & Feedback

Testing Process

During the internship, I worked closely with my mentor to define usability goals and validate our design assumptions. Usability testing was conducted internally using annotated prototypes shared with stakeholders across departments.

Feedback sessions focused on functionality, interaction clarity, and cross-team accessibility. I iterated on key flows based on direct stakeholder reviews and design critiques.

Key Feedback

  • Reduce user confusion by surfacing progress and system state clearly across steps.
  • Ensure components are reusable across product lines without custom overrides.
  • Consider accessibility and latency across global regions during design handoff.
