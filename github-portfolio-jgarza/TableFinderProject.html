Objectives

User Goals:

  • Quickly find restaurants that match personal preferences

Project Goals:

  • Simplify the reservation process for mobile users
  • Improve visual clarity and accessibility in UI

04 Discovery

To get a better understanding of what users needed in a booking tool, I conducted qualitative interviews with students and working professionals. I asked about their past experiences booking online and what they wished could be better.

A common thread? Frustration with unclear reservation processes, bad layouts, and inconsistent feedback. People just wanted something clean and fast. These insights shaped my early thinking.

I mapped out three key user flows: Discovering restaurants, Booking a table, and Checking in. My wireframes focused on minimal taps, clear actions, and soft UI touches.

06 Testing

I conducted usability testing with classmates and friends to uncover friction points in the booking flow. Testers appreciated the clean layout and quick navigation, but some struggled to find the confirmation screen.

Based on their feedback, I improved button visibility, clarified page transitions, and added microinteractions to confirm actions. This iterative cycle helped refine both the functionality and the visual language of the app.
