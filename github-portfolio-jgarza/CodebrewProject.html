White Paper Research

To understand the current landscape of coding education, I analyzed several white papers and academic articles focusing on online learning platforms. The research highlighted key challenges such as high dropout rates, lack of engagement, and accessibility issues.

Competitive Audit

I evaluated existing platforms like Codecademy, Udemy, and freeCodeCamp to identify their strengths and weaknesses. While these platforms offer extensive content, they often lack personalized learning paths and community engagement.

CodeBrew Caf√© aims to bridge these gaps by providing tailored learning experiences and fostering a supportive community for learners.

User Interviews

Conducted interviews with 10 individuals, including students, professionals, and self-taught developers. Common pain points included overwhelming content, lack of motivation, and difficulty in finding reliable resources.

These interviews informed the need for a platform that offers structured learning paths, motivational elements, and curated content.

Personas

Based on the research, I developed two primary personas:

  • Alex, 22: A college student seeking a structured and engaging way to learn coding.
  • Jordan, 35: A working professional looking to switch careers into tech with flexible learning options.

These personas guided the design decisions to cater to diverse learning needs and schedules.

Key Themes

  • Accessibility: Ensuring content is available to learners with varying abilities and resources.
  • Engagement: Incorporating interactive elements to maintain learner interest.
  • Community: Building a supportive network for peer-to-peer learning and motivation.

These themes became the pillars of CodeBrew Caf√©'s design and functionality.

üé® Design Area

Design Principles

I developed guiding principles grounded in clarity, motivation, and friendliness:

  • Keep it Simple: Prioritize clarity by removing visual clutter and focusing the UI on key actions.
  • Progress, Not Pressure: Let users move at their own pace, bookmark lessons, and feel encouraged.
  • Feel Friendly: Soften the experience with rounded edges, warm hues, and casual language.

User Flows

I mapped out essential flows to reduce friction and support student needs:

  • Find a Tutorial: Discover content quickly via search or topic browsing.
  • Bookmark Progress: Save and resume lessons easily.
  • Browse by Category: Sort by type (video, interactive, article) for learning preference alignment.

Sketches & Wireframes

I started with rough hand-drawn sketches to explore layouts and hierarchies. After testing basic layouts with peers, I translated them into grayscale wireframes in Figma.

The focus was on clarity and accessibility ‚Äî ensuring all primary actions were within easy thumb reach and labeled with clear language.

High-Fidelity UI

The final interface embraces simplicity, color intentionality, and inclusive typography. Components are designed with both light and dark modes in mind.

Key actions use accent colors for visibility, while spacing and contrast promote accessibility and legibility.
