Competitive Audit

I analyzed platforms like Codecademy, freeCodeCamp, and Scrimba to evaluate their accessibility, layout, and how they guide users through content. While some offered a clean onboarding flow, others were cluttered or overwhelming, especially for beginners.

I noticed a lack of personalization, inconsistent navigation patterns, and limited mobile optimization ‚Äî key gaps I aimed to improve with CodeBrew Caf√©.

White Paper Research

I dug into published research around digital learning for underrepresented students. Several findings highlighted the importance of:

  • Progressive skill reinforcement
  • Low-friction interfaces
  • Consistent UI patterns for cognitive retention

These insights grounded my early decisions and pushed me toward clean, focused layouts with minimal distractions.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with 4 students across different backgrounds ‚Äî all of whom had tried learning to code online. Their feedback aligned with my audit: tutorials were often too advanced, too disorganized, or too expensive.

Most users wanted bite-sized learning, intuitive navigation, and the ability to "bookmark" where they left off.

Personas

From the research, I created two main personas ‚Äî one being a college student juggling part-time work, the other a self-taught developer switching careers. These personas helped me stay grounded in real user needs as I moved into design.

Key Themes

  • Focus: Users needed one app that helps them focus on just the right material at the right time.
  • Control: They wanted to choose their pace and pick up where they left off.
  • Progress: They craved visible milestones and feedback as they learned.

üé® Design Area

Design Principles

With my research insights in mind, I framed three guiding principles:

  • Keep it Simple: Strip down visual noise and surface what matters.
  • Progress, Not Pressure: Let users move at their pace, bookmark lessons, and celebrate milestones.
  • Feel Friendly: Soften the experience with cozy UI touches ‚Äî rounded edges, warm colors, and conversational microcopy.

User Flows

I mapped out key flows ‚Äî including "Find a Tutorial," "Bookmark Progress," and "Browse by Category." Each one was designed to minimize taps and reduce decision fatigue.

Sketches & Wireframes

I started with quick hand-drawn sketches to shape layout ideas. From there, I moved into grayscale wireframes in Figma to block out structure and component hierarchy before layering in color or imagery.

High-Fidelity UI

The final UI is clean and purposefully sparse ‚Äî built for mobile-first, low-distraction learning. Color is used intentionally to guide the eye and emphasize actions. Typography is legible and friendly, with accessible contrast ratios across modes.

Usability Testing Approach

I ran 3 rounds of unmoderated testing with peers and students who regularly used coding tools. I asked them to complete tasks like finding a tutorial, saving a lesson, and returning to a bookmarked section.

Participants used mobile and tablet devices ‚Äî and I observed behaviors like hesitation, drop-off points, and confusion with icon meanings.

Key Feedback

  • ‚ÄúContinue Watching‚Äù wasn‚Äôt clearly tappable ‚Äî felt like a static label.
  • The search bar blended too much with the background.

What I Changed

  • Made CTAs bolder and used visual affordances (shadows, hover states).
  • Increased visual hierarchy of the top nav and search field.
  • Added subtle iconography for content types, improving scan-ability.

These changes led to clearer task flows and reduced time-to-completion in follow-up tests.
