UX/UI Teardown

User Interviews

We conducted interviews with 5 students who use fast food apps regularly. We asked about their priorities when ordering, how they engage with rewards, and common frustrations. Top issues included hard-to-navigate menus, unclear order status, and overly busy home screens.

Key Themes

  • Rewards: People liked rewards systems, but only when they are easy to track and redeem.

üé® Design Area

Design Principles

After synthesizing our research, we crafted three guiding principles for the redesign:

  • Less Noise, More Crave: Let bold product visuals and a clean layout do the heavy lifting ‚Äî reduce promotional clutter.
  • Guide the Craving: Improve hierarchy and predictability so users always know where to go next.
  • Fast, Focused, Familiar: Mimic the pace and familiarity of in-store ordering while keeping the flow flexible and mobile-optimized.

User Flows

We redesigned the core flows for three scenarios:

  • Quick Reorder: Access previous orders from the home screen and repeat in two taps.
  • Rewards Redemption: Redeem available rewards in the cart without interrupting the checkout flow.
  • Meal Customization: Simplified ingredient selectors and smart groupings to reduce scroll and tap fatigue.

Our flows emphasized confidence and speed ‚Äî always showing progress and next steps clearly.
